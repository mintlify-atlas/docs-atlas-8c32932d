RatingFlow's negative feedback shield automatically intercepts dissatisfied customer responses before they become public reviews, giving you the opportunity to address concerns privately and protect your online reputation.

## How the shield works

When customers rate their experience with your business, RatingFlow uses intelligent sentiment routing to determine where that feedback should go.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Customer provides rating">
    Your customer receives a review request via SMS, email, or QR code and rates their experience on a scale.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sentiment detection">
    RatingFlow analyzes the rating in real-time. Ratings of 1-3 stars trigger the negative feedback shield.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Private routing">
    Instead of being directed to Google, Facebook, or other public platforms, the customer is redirected to a private internal feedback form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Direct resolution">
    You receive the feedback privately and can address the complaint directly with the customer, potentially saving the relationship.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  The negative feedback shield prevents low ratings from permanently damaging your public star rating while still giving customers a voice.
</Info>

## Why this protects your brand

Public negative reviews can have lasting consequences for your business:

- **Permanent visibility**: Once published, negative reviews remain visible to all potential customers
- **SEO impact**: Lower average ratings reduce your visibility in local search results
- **Revenue loss**: Studies show businesses with ratings below 4.0 stars generate significantly less revenue
- **Unfair criticism**: Emotional or unreasonable complaints can't be prevented on public platforms

<Warning>
  Without a negative feedback shield, a single bad experience can result in a permanent 1-star review that impacts your business for years.
</Warning>

## Benefits of private feedback resolution

By routing negative feedback privately, you gain several advantages:

### Direct customer communication

You can reach out to dissatisfied customers immediately to understand their concerns and offer solutions, refunds, or apologies before they escalate.

### Relationship recovery

Many customers who initially had a negative experience can become loyal advocates if you address their issues properly and promptly.

### Brand protection

Your public-facing star rating remains high, ensuring that first impressions for potential customers are positive.

### Fair filtering

You can identify and address legitimate complaints while preventing disproportionate damage from overly emotional or unreasonable feedback.

<Info>
  The negative feedback shield doesn't hide all negative feedback—it simply creates a private channel for resolution before issues go public.
</Info>

## Integration with review platforms

The shield works seamlessly with your review collection workflow:

- **Automated requests**: Works with bulk SMS, email, and QR code campaigns
- **Real-time routing**: Instant sentiment analysis ensures proper direction
- **Consistent experience**: Customers receive a smooth experience regardless of their rating
- **Multi-platform support**: Protects your reputation across Google, Facebook, Trustpilot, and more

## Best practices

To maximize the effectiveness of your negative feedback shield:

1. **Respond quickly**: Set up notifications so you can address private feedback within hours, not days
2. **Be empathetic**: Acknowledge the customer's frustration and show genuine concern
3. **Offer solutions**: Provide concrete remedies like refunds, discounts, or service recovery
4. **Follow up**: After resolving the issue, consider asking if they'd be willing to share their updated experience publicly

<Warning>
  Never pressure customers to remove or change legitimate negative feedback. Focus on genuine resolution and relationship repair.
</Warning>
