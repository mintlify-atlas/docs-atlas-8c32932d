## Overview

QR code review management is perfect for physical businesses like restaurants, hotels, and retail stores. Generate custom QR codes that allow customers to scan and review instantly on their mobile devices.

QR codes eliminate friction in the review collection process by providing an immediate, contactless way for customers to share their feedback.

## How QR code reviews work

RatingFlow's QR code system integrates the same smart-routing technology used in SMS and email campaigns:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Customer scans the QR code">
    Using their smartphone camera, customers scan your custom QR code from receipts, table tents, signage, or other materials.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Mobile-optimized rating page loads">
    The customer is taken to a mobile-friendly page where they select their star rating.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Smart routing activates">
    - **4-5 stars:** Customer is redirected to Google Maps, Facebook, Trustpilot, or your chosen public review platform
    - **1-3 stars:** Customer is directed to a private feedback form where you can address their concerns directly
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The QR code acts as a 5 star reviews generator by ensuring only satisfied customers reach public platforms, while giving you the opportunity to resolve issues with dissatisfied customers privately.
</Note>

## Best practices for QR code deployment

<Tip>
  Position QR codes at eye level and in well-lit areas for maximum visibility and ease of scanning.
</Tip>

### Design considerations

- **Size matters:** Ensure your QR code is large enough to be easily scanned (minimum 2x2 inches recommended)
- **High contrast:** Use dark codes on light backgrounds for optimal scanning reliability
- **Include instructions:** Add text like "Scan with your phone's camera" to guide customers
- **Brand it:** Customize the design to match your brand aesthetic while maintaining scannability

### Placement strategy

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify touchpoints">
    Map out every customer interaction point where feedback would be valuable.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Prioritize high-intent moments">
    Focus on locations where customers have just completed a positive experience.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test visibility">
    Ensure QR codes are clearly visible and accessible in your chosen locations.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Optimize based on data">
    Review which locations generate the most scans and adjust your strategy accordingly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Industry-specific use cases

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Restaurants">
    Place QR codes on table tents, receipts, and takeout menus. Encourage customers to scan before leaving or while waiting for their food.
    
    <Tip>
      In restaurants, place QR codes near the exit to catch customers who just had a great meal and are most likely to leave positive feedback.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Hotels">
    Display QR codes at the front desk, in guest rooms, and in common areas. Include them in checkout materials.
    
    Consider placing codes in:
    - Welcome folders in guest rooms
    - Next to the concierge desk
    - In elevators or lobby areas
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Retail stores">
    Add QR codes to receipts, shopping bags, and point-of-sale displays. Place signage near the entrance or checkout.
    
    <Note>
      Retail businesses can also include QR codes in product packaging for post-purchase feedback collection.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Service providers">
    Include QR codes on business cards, invoices, and service completion paperwork.
    
    Service providers can also:
    - Display codes in waiting rooms
    - Include them in follow-up materials
    - Add them to vehicle signage
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
